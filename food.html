Food in India

These are some of my favourite Indian foods!

Traditional Indian Breakfast

A typical breakfast in India varies depending on region, but is often quite similar to a lunch or dinner. A breakfast plate in India might include roti (flatbread), dosas (thin crepes made of lentils) or idlis (steamed rice-dough pancakes), and different dips and chutneys, as well as spiced potatoes.Often there will be a grilled tomato as well as mushrooms, and baked beans.And of course, no breakfast is complete without a lovely pot of tea!

Indian Food & Beverages

Puri, Khaja, Pongal, Misal, Ras Malai & Rasgulla.

  1. Puri is an unleavened, fried Indian bread made with water, finer or coarser wheat flour, and occasionally cumin seeds.
  2. Khaja is a traditional Indian dessert consisting of flour, sugar, and ghee-based dough that is deep-fried in oil until golden and crispy.
  3. Pongal is a sweet rice dish that is usually eaten during special or ceremonial occasions in Sri Lanka.
  4. Ras malai is a popular Indian dessert consisting of white cream, sugar, milk, and cardamom-flavored paneer cheese known as chhana.

Bangers and Mash

This classic of sausages, mashed potato and gravy is not unique to Ireland, but what makes it special is the Irish sausages. Most countries have their own way of making sausages, and they are one thing I miss from home if I'm away travelling!

Bacon and Cabbage

I couldn't possibly make a list of Irish food without including this very traditional dish!

It might not sound very interesting, but this hearty meal of boiled ham, potatoes and green cabbage is tasty and filling. I love to smother the potatoes in butter, and I also like a little mustard with the bacon.

I like to make it extra special by cooking the cabbage in the water that the ham was boiled in.

If there are any leftovers you can make another one of my favourites: fried cabbage!
